Our Dataset: Gutenberg Books Metadata

What is in Our Dataset?

We are working with metadata from Project Gutenberg books. Basically, it is information about thousands of free digital books. For this exercise we used just a subset of 99 book records. We got this from a Kaggle dataset.

Quick Stats
  • 99 book records
  • 7 data columns

Dataset Structure

Here is what each column in our data contains:

Field What It Contains
ID Unique number for each book
Type Text, dataset, audio, etc.
Issued When the book was published
Title The book's name
Language What language it is written in
Authors Who wrote it
Subjects Topic categories (this is what we analyze!)

Why This Dataset?

Our Reasoning

We picked Gutenberg books because they have really detailed subject tags. Perfect for testing our subject-counting algorithm!

Quality vs Quantity

We decided it was better to have clean, reliable data than lots of messy data.

What are We Using It For

This dataset allows us to develop and validate an algorithm that identifies popular subjects within book collections. By working with this focused dataset, we can refine our approach before scaling to larger collections. When combined with temporal data, our algorithm could potentially reveal trending subjects and patterns in literature over time.

Note: We are using this data under fair use for educational analysis as part of our university assignment.
